Abstract
In both developed and developing countries, the oil and gas industries are considered to be one of the most
significant industries in terms of its impact on health and safety of the working population. Oil and Gas
industries are both economically and socially important. However, the oil and gas industries are also
recognized to be the most hazardous. This research was set out to assess accident rate, safety performance
and compliance in three different sectors of oil and gas producing companies in Nigeria (indigenous,
national and international oil and gas industries) and to know DPR standard of safety for the oil and gas
company.
The study populations were 474 staff of the three different sectors of oil and gas companies and 96 for
DPR. 700 questionnaires were designed and administered. The response rate was 81.43%. The collected
data was analysed descriptively to determine accident rate amongst the three different oil and gas sectors
and using Kendall’s coefficient of concordance to establish the level of correspondent of the respondents
on awareness and compliance to safety standard and procedure.
The study revealed that the respondents of indigenous oil and gas companies has low safety standard
when compared to DPR standard, low level of awareness (44%) and compliance (47%) to safety standard
and procedures and high accident rate, national oil and gas companies respondents revealed safety

INTRODUCTION
Exploration and production of oil and gas is technically challenging, involving different stages and
processes. First, exploration companies have to tap into unseen pressurised underground resources to
bring oil and gas to the surface. Extracted products are then transported to refineries over long distances,
often via pressurized pipelines or large ocean tankers. The refining and transformation of these products
through complicated chemical processes are then carried out under high temperature and pressure, hence
making the process a very hazardous one. Complex processes at various stages in oil and gas production
are usually managed by people and are highly subject to errors which could result in accidents or disaster.
One of such disasters is the Piper Alpha 1988 disaster which resulted in the death of 167 men and marks

affect an organization’s value and the confidence of its employees, therefore, safety certainly is good
business. Therefore, the issue of safety has been central to many studies carried out about oil and gas
workplace environments.In the past, accidents in the oil and gas industry were commonly attributed to
technological malfunctioning, while the human elements present in these accidents were usually ignored.
With improvement in technology and subsequent reduction in technological failures, the role of human
error especially in terms of safety performance has become more obvious. This shift in focus is driven by
the awareness that organisational, managerial and human factors rather than purely technical failures are
prime causes of accidents in industries. Skogdalen and others, (2012) cited human error from poor safety
performance to be the cause of most industrial accidents both past and present. Esso Australia’s fire
explosion for one was attributed to a misjudged safety decision to introduce warm liquid into a cold pipe.
STUDY BACKGROUND
The Oil and Gas industry is a major contributor to the Nigeria economy. It accounts for about 90% of the
federal government’s annuals revenue. This industry has dominated by foreign interests and major
activities like exploration, drilling, production; well intervention and servicing provision remain primarily
controlled by IOCs, NOCs, and local contractors. According to an IEA report on energy consumption in
2010, oil and gas provides the world’s population of about 6.9 billion people with 90% of their daily
energy need. Despite the huge benefits this industry brings to society, it has been noted to be an industry
characterised by complex exploration and production processes, which if not well managed, could lead to
disasters. Considering the nature of the Oil and Gas industry and the complex processes involved in its
operation, described it as a high risk industry.
The oil and gas industry involves the interaction of technical, organisational, managerial, environmen-
tal and human factors; fall out of which leads to accidents. in reviewing the Macondo disaster which
claimed the lives of 11 people and resulted in a spill of about 4.9 million barrels of crude oil, came out
with important conclusions. The study not only established the immediate cause of the incident, but also
revealed the role of lack of continuous risk assessment from company management, as well as lack of
timely risk based decision making, in the occurrence of the accident. Poor risk assessment/management
among other human errors, have been identified as a major cause of accidents in the oil and gas industry.
Apart from the Macondo disaster of 2010, another major incident in the oil and gas industry that
highlighted the role of poor safety performance leading to accident occurrence was the Texas refinery
disaster of 2010 (CSB 2007). As reported by the chemical safety board investigation into the refinery
explosion, management of the refinery failed to properly assess and manage the safety implication of
sitting contractor trailers close to the ISOM plant (Khan and others 2007). This failure by management
was noted to have played a major role in the occurrence of that accident. The investigation highlighted
the need for the adoption of this safety culture in the industry. Arguably, the issue of safety culture has
since been a prime consideration within the industry with every organisation striving to embrace this
culture. Safety standards and procedure exists for the oil and gas industries but with no proper
implementation. It is not unusual to find that while some employees are totally committed to safety
improvement efforts, some others pay lip service to it. However, through persistent involvement effort and
peer influence, it is likely that more attitudes can be changed. In line with this research study, many
accident or incident that occurs in the oil and gas industry is as a result of poor or inadequate safety
performance, while others are due to non-adherence of workers to work procedures as well as safety
measures. The workers must realize that they play an important role, contributing in the accomplishment
of the job task. The awareness and compliance of workers towards a safe and healthy working
environment is an important aspect in fostering an overall better working condition for the workers
themselves.

SAFETY PERFRORMANCE EVALUATION OBJECTIVES
The objectives of the study are:
●To investigate safety procedure, regulations, policies and accident rate related to the oil and gas
operators in Nigeria.
●To determine the level of awareness and compliance to the safety standard and procedure in
selected oil and gas companies within Niger Delta.
●To verify the DPR standard on the level of awareness and compliance to safety for oil and gas
industries.
●To develop bases for evaluation of safety compliance and audit.
●To provide suggestions on the best ways to adhere to the safety standards and procedures.
RESEARCH DELIVERABLES
The adherence to safety standards and procedures cannot be overemphasized. Non-adherence to these
standards results in economic or financial loss as well as human and material disabilities or loss. This
study was carried out to:
●See how well the awareness and compliance is as regarding adhering to Safety standards and
procedures in the different selected oil and gas operating companies.
●Bring to awareness DPR standard in maintaining good the Safety standards in oil and gas
industries.
●To provide ways for better compliance to the safety standards in oil and gas industries
RESEARCH BENEFITS TO OIL AND GAS OPERATION
●Establish the need for effective HSE management system in oil companies.
●To help reduce accident rate in operation sites
PROCEDURE FOR SAFETY INVESTIGATION
Two types of research method are used in this study. There are the qualitative and quantitative approaches.
They are used to compare the results in this study with other researches. Both types of research method
are used to realize the real safety problems and danger of injuries that occur in oil and gas industry, to
investigate safety awareness, procedures, policies, accident rate in oil and gas projects, compliance to
safety standard and procedures at oil and gas company projects site within the Niger delta region.
The questionnaire was designed to suit the projects within oil and gas operators Total E&P, SPDC and
MPN (INOC). ENANTO, Halden Nigeria limited and MB&C Investment (NOC), Encon Services, Fiddil
Limited and Platform Petroleum Limited (IOCs), on four categories of respondents, namely: Contractors,
Site Supervisors, Projects Managers and Workers. The questionnaire is also distributed to Department of
Petroleum Resources (regulatory agency) to determine their standard on safety awareness and compliance
for oil and gas industries. The questionnaire design was dependent on the nature of work and issues of
safety in oil and gas operations projects.

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Having existing policies and safety procedures without implementation is as good as not having it at all.
The results of this study indicates
●Indigenous oil and gas firms doing business has some framework on safety standard and
procedures, but poor guiding principles on proper performance and compliance.
●The national oil and gas companies have safety standards and procedures which are not effectively
implemented amongst its workers but are still on improving stage.
●International oil and gas companies have very high safety performance and compliance rate
amongst its respondents but require consistency to attain zero accident rates.
Penalties for defaulters are the major cause of non-compliance and negligence on the side of the
workers. But on the management perspective, proper implementation of safety standards and procedures
is a function of good preparedness and commitments. If the management is committed to safety as much
as toward the completion and delivery of the project within the specified time and cost, creating awareness
of their workers to safety standards and procedures and training toward effectiveness in safe practices will
reduce the cost and time of production and increase the company’s profit.
RECOMMENDATIONS
The following are some of the recommended checks to ensure that safety standards and procedures are
integrated into oil and gas companies operations especially for indigenous and national oil and gas
companies, to minimized accidents, injuries and casualties at workplace.
CONTRACTORS
●The contractors should train the workers, promote the safety culture for workers and
educate them on how to avoid the risk and use the personal protective equipment
properly while on operation site.
●The contractors should prepare the daily safety meeting (such as tool box meetings,
pep talks) during the work onsite visit.
●The contractor should make sure that all the workers wear the personal protection
equipment and punish the workers who make safety violation.
PROJECT MANAGERS
●The top management of the oil and gas firm should have safety policies in place and
ensure it implementation on site at all time during operation.
●Management of these firms should make these policies available to their workers in
an easy to understand language and format.
●Top management should make funds available to provide personal protective
equipment for their workers.
