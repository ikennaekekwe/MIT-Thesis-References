## 0. The headline finding: two axes, not one

Ferreira et al.'s 22 IKT elements and the six capability categories answer different questions and belong on different axes. Collapsing them would be a category error, and is the "forcing" to avoid.

- **Axis 1, the object of assurance (substance): what the operating capability *is*.** Six content categories (people and retained teams; operating routines; integrity and emergency systems; decision rights and accountability; data and asset history; contractor and technical-support architecture). This is what gets disclosed, absorbed, and verified. → **visible Table 5.2.**
- **Axis 2, the transfer-process specification (mechanism): how the handover must be *designed and executed*.** Ferreira's 22 elements are determinants of transfer success (motivation, absorptive capacity, training, routines, coordination, management capacity), organized by Ferreira into pretransition / transition / post-transition phases that map directly onto the framework's lifecycle. → **Appendix H**, and the source of the transfer-design obligations in 5.4.

**Verdict:** the six content categories all land cleanly on Axis 1; Ferreira's 22 elements all land cleanly on Axis 2 (lifecycle x role); **zero** Ferreira elements belong on Axis 1. The earlier v2 phrasing ("each Table 5.2 cell carries a Ferreira element") conflated the two and is corrected here.

**One structural consequence.** Ferreira is a bilateral seller-buyer study with no regulator. The regulator role is therefore **not Ferreira-derived**; it is an overlay that *verifies* the seller-buyer transfer-process elements, grounded in the legal hooks (PIA, NUPRC Reg., NOGICD), the externality argument, and the comparative models (NSTA / Norway / Alberta). The worksheet marks regulator obligations as verification overlays, not as Ferreira elements.

**Axis 2 forcing-check:** all 22 land in the lifecycle x role matrix without strain, because Ferreira *is* a lifecycle, two-party study. Distribution: Pre-transition (diagnosis/design) ~10, Transition (execution) ~9, Post (verification) ~3, which confirms Ferreira's own observation that most failures are planning failures and validates front-loading the framework's diagnosis and transfer-design phases. Roles: Joint coordination dominates (governance + management-capacity themes), Seller motivation (3-4), Buyer absorptive capacity (5-6); the regulator appears only as a verification overlay on element 17 (and, by extension, verifying that a compliant transfer plan exists). **No element belongs on Axis 1.** Elements 7, 19, and 4/21 carry the strongest evidentiary load (seller availability, data access, time-bounded transition), and each maps to a Chapter 4 finding and a statute. Roughly nine elements are "Ferreira-grounded transfer-plan content" with no direct statute; under the prescriptive-citation rule (every prescriptive sentence cites a Chapter 4 finding, a legal hook, *or* a Ferreira element/comparative provision), a Ferreira citation is sufficient warrant for these.

## 4. Corrections this gate forces in the v2 architecture (for v3)

1. **5.3 gap statement (material).** Replace "the rules do not assess technical capacity" with the precise residual: NUPRC Reg. 7 already assesses technical capacity, financial capacity, D&A, host-community/remediation funds, labour, data repatriation, and director fitness, and permits competency site visits (Reg. 7(4)); the residual gap is that technical capacity is assessed at the **corporate/acquirer** level, the competency visit is **discretionary**, there is **no seller-disclosure duty** for embedded capability, **no required structured transfer**, and **no post-transfer verification**. The punchline survives: no provision requires demonstrating asset-specific **well-control readiness**.
2. **FHN/Afren reframed** as a **pre-2024 historical** illustration of a fitness gap the 2024 regulation has **partly closed** (Reg. 7(5)(a)(iv) now screens director convictions), not a current gap. This strengthens the thesis (the direction of reform validates the diagnosis) and avoids an overclaim.
3. **Table 5.2 reconceived** as the six content categories (Axis 1), with Ferreira's 22 elements moved to **Appendix H** (Axis 2). Update A.2 (5.2/5.4) and A.3 accordingly.
4. **Regulator role** explicitly marked as a verification overlay, not Ferreira-derived (update 5.4.1).
5. The Reg. 7(4) competency-visit power and Reg. 7(2)(g) data-repatriation duty are **stronger existing hooks** than v2 used; foreground them in 5.4/5.5 (the framework makes the discretionary visit mandatory and asset-specific for higher-risk transfers, and extends data repatriation into a structured asset-history disclosure).
