# Document-as-Agent: Function versus Authorial Proxy
### Expansion of Review and Editing Protocol, Pass 5

## The rule
A document or construct (the framework, the design, the chapter, a section, a table, a figure, an appendix, the analysis, the loop, the apparatus, the model) may stand as the grammatical subject of a verb only when it performs its own function, holds its own state, appears in passive or navigational form, or reports what evidence shows. It may not stand as the subject of a verb that names an act of authorship: reasoning, claiming, defining, or constructing. In those cases the construct is impersonating the author, and the sentence must be recast.

## The test
Imagine the framework enacted and operating in the world, with the author absent. Ask whether the verb describes something the artifact does in operation, or something the author did to build or justify it.

- If the enacted artifact can still do it on its own, the verb names a **function**. Keep the construct as subject. An enacted framework can require, verify, screen, protect, close a gap, scale, and tie security to an assessment without its author present.
- If the verb makes sense only as the author's act, the construct is an **authorial proxy**. Convert. No enacted framework re-specifies a variable, argues, establishes, grounds a proposal, or adds a component; those are things the author did in designing or defending it.

Blunt cross-check: rewrite the clause with "the author" as subject. If "the author re-specifies, argues, establishes, grounds, adds" is the true meaning, the construct was a proxy. If "the author verifies, scales, ties" is plainly wrong because the framework is what does those things, it was a function.

## Keep (function, state, location, reporting, navigation)
- **Function verbs:** requires, verifies, screens, gates, tests, detects, protects, closes a gap, ties, sets thresholds or targets, installs, asks something of a party, obliges, mandates, applies to, covers, scales, extends to or into (scope), presupposes, tolerates, hinges on, rests on, prevents, allows.
- **State and copula:** is warranted, is bounded, is calibrated, is two-sided.
- **Passive and location:** is set out in Section 5.4, is organized in Table 5.3, is shown in Figure 5.1.
- **Empirical reporting, where the subject is data and not a document:** the evidence shows, the cases divide on this point, the content analysis codes ten elements, the data indicate. Distinguish "the evidence shows" (data) from "the analysis shows" (the author's reasoning); the second converts.
- **Navigation, the roadmap and cross-reference exception:** Section 5.6 develops those requirements; Chapter 2 develops the structure from Akerlof; set out next in Section 5.4. This covers pointing the reader to where material sits. It does not license a substantive claim dressed as navigation: "this chapter argues that X" is a proxy, not a roadmap.
- **Logical entailment between propositions:** the case for intervention follows from the findings; a specific design follows from that diagnosis.

## Convert (authorial proxy)
Verbs of reasoning or construction attributed to a construct: argues, claims, contends, shows (as in "the analysis shows"), demonstrates, establishes, proves, finds, identifies, defines, specifies a definition, re-specifies, grounds an argument or proposal, adds a component in construction, introduces a concept, maps, traces, motivates, examines, explores, considers, addresses a concern, reveals, sets out to, aims to, seeks to.

Two verbs sit on the boundary and are decided by the test, not by the word:
- **extends:** "extends assurance into post-transfer verification" is scope (keep); "extends the mapping to ten more elements" is analysis (convert).
- **adds:** "what the framework adds over the baseline is specification and closure" is marginal contribution (borderline; prefer *contributes*); "the design adds the buyer's absorptive capacity" is construction (convert).

## Fix approach, in order of preference
1. **Recast as the artifact's function, keeping the construct as subject.** "The framework re-specifies the variable" becomes "the framework requires demonstration of the asset-specific variable," or "under the framework, that variable becomes the object of disclosure and verification."
2. **Passive with the author implied.** The active-voice default yields here, because removing false agency is the higher rule. "That variable is re-specified here as X"; "the mapping is extended in Appendix H."
3. **Make the proposition or question the subject,** for transitions and entailments. "A specific institutional design follows from that diagnosis"; "the question now becomes one of design."

Not a fix: swapping one construct for another. "The thesis argues" to "this chapter argues" changes nothing.

## Reference cases: converted

| Original (proxy) | Recast | Why it was a proxy |
|---|---|---|
| the framework re-specifies that variable, adds the disclosure, and grounds each obligation | that variable is re-specified here, with the disclosure added to it and each obligation grounded in a hook | re-specify, add, and ground are design acts the author performs |
| the design grounds those proposals, adds capacity, and re-specifies hooks | the design is grounded in a theory; it goes beyond those proposals by incorporating capacity and working through hooks | grounding an argument and re-specifying are authorship |
| the analysis now turns from explanation to design | the question now becomes one of design | the author turns; the analysis does not |
| the diagnosis motivates a specific design | a specific institutional design follows from that diagnosis | entailment, not a rhetorical act by the diagnosis |

## Why it matters
The chapter's authority depends on two things staying visible and separate: the author's reasoning, which must read as reasoning a person did and will defend, and the framework's operation, which must read as something an enacted design would do. Collapsing them makes the framework appear to argue for its own adoption, which is at once an AI-texture tell and an epistemic confusion, because a design cannot supply the warrant for itself.

## One setting to fix in advance
The disputable case is empirical reporting with a construct-adjacent subject, above all "the evidence shows." This rule keeps it, on the ground that the subject is data rather than a document. If the standard for this thesis is stricter, convert it to "the cases show" or "incident rates rose" and apply that choice everywhere rather than case by case.
