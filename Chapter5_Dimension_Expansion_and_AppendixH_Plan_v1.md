## 1. The contribution, sharpened by the NUPRC framework

Narrowing the framework to production and safety would understate the thesis's contribution. The decisive finding from chasing the sources: the value dimensions worth adding are **not idiosyncratic**, they map onto the **seven cardinal pillars of NUPRC's own Upstream Asset Divestment and Exit Guidance Framework** (technical capacity; financial viability; legal compliance; decommissioning and abandonment; host community trust and environmental remediation; industrial relations and labour; data repatriation), applied to the Oando, Chappal, Seplat/MPNU, and Renaissance consents, with over US$400 million in pre-sale D&A liabilities secured via escrow and letters of credit in 2024.

This sharpens the contribution to a claim the evidence supports and a reviewer will respect:

> Capability loss threatens the **full set of national value dimensions that NUPRC's framework already recognizes** (financial, decommissioning, community, environment, labour, data), **plus production performance**, which the framework assumes the technical-capacity pillar delivers but never separately verifies. NUPRC's pillars are assessed at consent as corporate-financial and compliance attributes; Chapter 4 shows that operators cleared on those attributes still diverged on production and integrity, because the **embedded sociotechnical operating capability** that actually drives these outcomes is not what the pillars measure, and nothing verifies it after close. The thesis therefore (i) reframes capability loss as multi-dimensional value at risk, (ii) adds the production dimension, (iii) re-specifies the technical-capacity assessment around demonstrated operating capability, and (iv) closes the loop with post-transfer verification across these dimensions.

That is a genuine contribution to the literature: a capability-grounded, multi-dimensional, lifecycle-verified reframing of an existing regulatory framework, not a safety checklist.

**Calibration (mandatory, per the standing overclaiming guard):**
- **Demonstrated in this thesis** (the critical experiment + cross-case validation): V1 production, V2 integrity. These are the two dependent-variable dimensions.

## 3. How Table 5.1 relates to Table 5.2 (and where Production appears twice, correctly)

- **Table 5.1 = value dimensions at risk** (outcomes). Production is a row here as the contributed dimension.
- **Table 5.2 = the capability bundle** (what is disclosed/absorbed/verified): the four **RBPS pillars** (operational-integrity capability) **plus a Production and subsurface operating-capability row** (reservoir/well/production-optimization capability). Production appears here too, as a capability, which is the natural place for the "production row" since it is both an outcome (V1) and an operating capability that must be assured.
- **A capability-to-value mapping** (a figure, or a section of Appendix H) connects them: the same bundle protects multiple dimensions, e.g., asset-integrity capability protects V2 + V3 + V4; subsurface/production capability protects V1 + V7; people and competency protect all; stakeholder/community capability protects V5 + V8. This mapping is the mechanism by which a multi-dimensional contribution rests on a single capability construct, which keeps the thesis coherent rather than sprawling.

This satisfies both of your instincts: Production becomes a row (in both tables, each correctly), and the framework's contribution broadens to the full value set without collapsing into safety or losing calibration.
