## Important correction to the earlier (markdown-based) reconciliation

The earlier session, working from lossy markdown, read OML 26 at about 10 kb/d for 2022
and 2023 and called it agreement with Wood Mackenzie. The clean PDFs show those ~3,700 to

## Reporting-basis rules (resolved across batches)

- Operators (Aiteo, Eroton, Newcross, Shoreline/HEOSL) report GROSS in the per-company
  tables (confirmed via 2018 Table 2.5, whose "Total Production" equals Table 2.6's
  "Fiscalised Production"). Combined "NPDC FHN" rows (2020 Table 30) are gross.
- For NPDC alternative-funding JVs where only the partner name appears (2014, 2018), the
  figure is the 45% share; gross = share / 0.45.
- Terminology drifts: 2020 calls the headline "Fiscalised Production"; 2021-2023 separate
  "Metered Production at Flow Station" (gross) from "Fiscalized Production" (net of losses).
- Crude is in bbls; gas is in mmscf. Verify the unit before trusting a figure.

The series agree in 2016-2017 and 2020-2021 but diverge sharply in 2022-2023. NEITI is
delivered/metered (and OML 26 was hit by heavy deferment, 7.8 kb/d shut-in in 2021 alone);
Wood Mackenzie models capacity/entitlement and shows a smooth rise. For commercial-outcome
arguments the primary regulatory (NUPRC-signed-off) NEITI figures are the better measure;
they say OML 26 reached about 10 kb/d only briefly in 2020-2021 then relapsed to 3.7 to
6.2 in 2022-2023.

## Observation: final state (third revision stands)

The "OML 26 uniquely declined" (batch 1) and "differential recovery" (batch 2) readings
were both discarded earlier. With the full series, the robust facts are: (1) OML 26 is the
cohort's smallest producer throughout, most simply a function of asset endowment; (2) its
actual production is volatile and frequently below modeled capacity, with two troughs
(2013-2017 and 2022-2023) and heavy deferment; (3) structurally, OML 26 sits in the NPDC
alternative-funding group where NPDC operates and First Hydrocarbon was a 45% funding and
technical partner that never operated, so production cannot measure FHN's operational
capability. This confirms the 4.4.2 framing of First Hydrocarbon as the regulatory-
screening and capital-constraints test, not a capability-via-production case.
