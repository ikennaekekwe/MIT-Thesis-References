Stakeholders from Colombia, Ghana, Indonesia and Zambia 
gathered in Oslo, Norway, in November 2023 to exchange good 
practices on implementing the Opening Extractives programme.
The strong results that have been shown by the programme 
as it concludes its first phase are the product of collaboration 
with reform-minded stakeholders and partners. Their 
dedication is reflected in these achievements. 
From January 2024, Opening Extractives moves into its  
second phase. Drawing on insights and learning from phase 
one, and informed by an independent evaluation, the  
programme will adapt to maximise its impact, forging further 
progress towards focused objectives that are most relevant to 
the risks citizens in resource-rich countries face in the energy 
transition. Priorities include increasing the availability and 
quality of beneficial ownership data and the use of such data 
in the application, award and holding of extractive licenses 
to mitigate governance risks through the energy transition. It 
will also aim to shape and inform national and international 
debate on governance risks in the energy transition, 
communicating knowledge and building capacity of national, 
regional and local partners, institutions and champions.
We are grateful to our anchor donor, the BHP Foundation, and 
to the United States Agency for International Development 
(USAID), which has co-funded Opening Extractives since 
September 2023, and to all our national and international 
partners who enable the programme to deliver change.

Foreword from the 
Executive Directors of the 
EITI and Open Ownership 
Since 2021, Opening Extractives has worked to accelerate the 
availability and use of beneficial ownership data in resource-
rich countries with the objective of enabling citizens to 
understand who benefits from their resources and, in turn, 
to maximise the benefits they enjoy from those resources. 
Beneficial ownership data is an important tool in the 
fight against corruption. Corruption remains prevalent 
in the extractive sector, and it is an increased risk 
as the sector races to meet increased demand for 
the minerals needed for the energy transition.
Over the last year, Opening Extractives has enabled  
governments and companies to disclose high-quality beneficial 
ownership data through targeted technical assistance to 
support countries in pursuing their reform agendas. It has 
helped maintain political commitment to technical reform, and 
it has supported stakeholders to deliver beneficial ownership 
registers; create the right legislation where needed; and build 
the capacity of those tasked with implementing reforms. 
Some of the programme’s achievements 
over the last 12 months include:
Leading workshops, training 
and mentoring investigative 
journalists in Armenia, Ghana 
and Zambia on the use of 
beneficial ownership data, 
which has contributed to their 
publishing well-researched 
articles on beneficial 
ownership transparency 
and resource governance.
Sharing new evidence on 
the international stage on 
the importance of beneficial 
ownership transparency to 
achieving a just energy transition.
The launch of a new register 
and the issuance of beneficial 
ownership regulations for 
domestic corporations in Liberia.
Supporting the creation of a 
legal framework for beneficial 
ownership declarations in Ghana; 
the production of structured data 
in Nigeria; and assistance on 
verification mechanisms in Armenia. 
Strengthening technical 
capacity among implementers 
through training in Argentina, 
Armenia, Indonesia, Liberia, 
Mongolia, Philippines and 
Senegal; regional peer exchange 
events in Anglophone Africa 
and Latin America; and the 
production and dissemination 
of guidance on database 
design and data integrity.  
Raising awareness of the 
importance of beneficial 
ownership transparency 
across all regions through 
media and civil society 
engagement in Armenia, 
Liberia, Mongolia and Zambia, 
which deepened citizens’ 
awareness of the importance 
of accessible and timely 
beneficial ownership data.

Ghana
In Ghana, Opening Extractives has largely focused on  
promoting the use of beneficial ownership data as a strategy  
to demonstrate the need for comprehensive disclosures.  
For example, four investigative projects were mentored by  
a reputable journalist with the objective of testing the  
data-use tools shared in training workshops. These activities 
enabled investigative stories to be published by journalists  
and covered by the Ghanaian media. They drew public 
attention to beneficial ownership in Ghana, covering 
diverse topics such as illegal fishing; unlicensed 
companies exploiting natural resources; and the failure 
to submit beneficial ownership disclosures. They 
further identified that a large-scale mining license 
was wrongly awarded in 2017 to an Australian-owned 
company whose beneficial owners were confirmed to 
have been previously involved in criminal activities. 
In addition to capacity-building efforts, Opening  
Extractives has provided technical support to government  
stakeholders, including the Office of the Registrar of 
Companies, law enforcement agencies and licensing bodies.  
Regular engagement has resulted in further demand for 
assistance to strengthen capacity to embed beneficial 
ownership data in due diligence processes. Furthermore, 
Opening Extractives has engaged with law enforcement 
authorities on the use of beneficial ownership and extractive 
sector data for the regulation of license allocations.  
This includes the use of beneficial ownership data to conduct 
thorough due diligence, tax compliance and revenue auditing, 
and investigate red flags in complex corporate structures.
Indonesia 
In 2018, the Indonesian government issued a presidential 
regulation and decree requiring companies to report 
their beneficial owners to the Ministry of Law through the 
company register and in accordance with their license 
application procedures. As a result of this progress, 
Opening Extractives identified an opportunity to help the 
Government of Indonesia in strengthening its license 
application process through the use of beneficial ownership 
data. A technical workshop conducted with the Ministry 
of Energy and Mineral Resources (MEMR) in September 
2023 contributed to building the capacity of the license 
authorities from the MEMR in understanding and identifying 
corruption risks in licensing; understanding global best 
practices in using beneficial ownership data for due 
diligence processes; and identifying areas for improvement 
of its existing data collection procedures and systems. 
The impact of Opening Extractives in Indonesia lies in 
supporting the alignment of the MEMR’s beneficial ownership 
transparency work with broader national objectives and 
initiatives in other ministries and organisations working on 
reforms, such as the Ministry of Law and Human Rights, 
Stranas PK, the Open Government Partnership (OGP) and 
the Ministry of National Development Planning. Opening 
Extractives is further helping Indonesia meet the  
requirements of the EITI Standard ahead of its EITI Validation 
in 2024. This includes technical support to ensure data 
comprehensiveness, data quality and systematic  
disclosure of beneficial ownership transparency. 	
 
“Knowing who owns the companies that operate in  
the oil, gas and mining sectors is key to strengthening natural 
resource governance. In Ghana, it has also proven to be significant 
in improving the investment climate and promoting responsible 
business conduct. Through programmes such as Opening 
Extractives, we hope to tackle beneficial ownership challenges 
posed by hidden ownership and improve on transparency in the 
governance of our natural resources.”
Argentina
Argentina hosted a range of activities, prioritising the use 
and understanding of beneficial ownership data as well as 
regulatory reforms. Partnerships, such as that formed with 
the Forum of Argentinian Journalism (Foro de Periodismo 
Argentino), helped focus the attention of the media and the 
public on the value of beneficial ownership information in 
investigations. A two-day capacity-building workshop for 
journalists on how to use beneficial ownership data from  
the extractive sector was an opportunity for journalists  
across the country to discuss relevant cases and offered  
a hands-on approach to search, analyse and  
compare information. 
In April 2023, the Foundation for the Development  
of Sustainable Policies (Fundación para el Desarrollo  
de Políticas Sustentables) and Red Ruido, a network of 
investigative journalists, open data experts and civil society 
organisations (CSOs), published an analysis of beneficial 
ownership information on companies engaged in lithium 
production in Argentina. This research received substantial 
media attention, and won the second place in the biggest 
investigative journalism contest in Argentina.
Opening Extractives further helped the EITI multi-stakeholder 
group (MSG) and the EITI national secretariat in Argentina 
organise forward-looking workshops to shape reforms of  
the regulatory framework for beneficial ownership 
transparency in the extractive sector. This support led  
to changes that will enable companies to disclose most  
of their beneficial ownership information to the authorities  
in EITI reporting. This development runs in parallel to a law  
that has already been passed in Congress and awaits its 
approval in the Senate, on the creation of a public registry  
of beneficial ownership information.

Armenia 
Armenia became one of the first two countries in  
the world to publish its data to Open Ownership’s Beneficial 
Ownership Data Standard (BODS) across all economic sectors, 
and it established an online register in 2021. There has been 
strong demand in the country for workshops on scaling up 
the use of beneficial ownership data, and the Open Ownership 
BODS notebooks analysis shows what can be revealed by 
interrogating this data. Despite political unrest, government, 
civil society and industry in Armenia remain committed to 
maintaining progress on ownership transparency.
With beneficial ownership reporting now mandatory across 
all sectors of the Armenian economy, electronic submissions 
of beneficial ownership declarations by companies to 
the Armenia Business Register began in February 2023. 
The Opening Extractives programme provided support to 
the Armenian government to establish clear and robust 
verification procedures for beneficial ownership information.
 
Opening Extractives has also increased awareness  
of beneficial ownership in Armenia through capacity- 
building workshops for civil society actors and journalists.  
These workshops were focused on beneficial ownership  
data use for investigative reporting and advocacy, and they 
were jointly organised with the Freedom of Information  
Center of Armenia (FOICA). The workshops helped journalists 
analyse and use publicly available data in investigations. 
Following these workshops, participants published seven 
investigative articles covering various sectors of the economy, 
including Armenia’s extractive sector. They were able to use 
beneficial ownership data and combine it with information 
on tax and royalty payments as well as politically exposed 
persons (PEPs) and procurement. FOICA also published an 
informative step-by-step video on how to search for  
beneficial ownership information in Armenia for use by 
journalists and civil society actors.

Senegal  
Aiming to become a leader in beneficial ownership transparency in the Francophone  
Africa region, the Ministry of Justice of Senegal signed up to Opening Extractives at the end  
of 2021. Senegal EITI further published a roadmap for beneficial ownership transparency and  
an inter-ministerial steering committee was created, including representatives from the Ministry  
of Justice, the Ministry of Energy and Petroleum, the Ministry of Mines and the Ministry of Finance. 
Senegal’s Ministry of Justice is further considering legislation on mandating the government 
to collect beneficial ownership information in a structured and standardised format; verify the 
information; and make it available both to government agencies as well as the public through  
a central register. 
Drawing further on the scoping assessment, Opening Extractives facilitated a workshop for 
companies to discuss the legal and technical framework of Senegal’s beneficial ownership 
register. The Opening Extractives team demonstrated how the quality of declarations could be 
improved to meet the Open Ownership Principle on Definition (on having a single definition of 
beneficial ownership) and the EITI guidelines. Further training was provided for Ministry of Justice 
professionals, law enforcement authorities, CSOs and media representatives on how to match 
beneficial ownership data with other data sources and how to use such data in investigations. 
Opening Extractives has contributed to building an environment for high-quality beneficial 
ownership disclosure in Senegal, and this has been achieved through the programme’s 
collaboration with Gainde 2000, the platform that hosts and maintains Senegal’s online register. 
Consultations with the Ministry of Justice and Gainde 2000 aim to establish a plan for digitising 
procedures for declaring beneficiaries. The programme will further assist in improvements in the 
management of beneficial ownership declarations in Senegal through technical capacity building.

Ecuador  
Ecuador announced it was joining Opening Extractives in April 2023 through the 
Superintendency of Companies, Securities and Insurance (Superintendencia 
de Compañías, Valores y Seguros). The Memorandum of Understanding 
between the Superintendency and Opening Extractives will push forward new 
disclosures of beneficial ownership information for the extractive sector and 
open opportunities for CSOs and other governmental authorities to engage 
in peer-learning opportunities, data-use workshops and data analysis. 
In 2024, Opening Extractives aims to identify priorities in the implementation 
of beneficial ownership transparency with the EITI multi-stakeholder group in 
Ecuador and move towards a public registry of companies linked to extractive 
activities. The programme will strengthen inter-institutional collaboration on 
beneficial ownership transparency through facilitating workshops and spaces for 
dialogue to identify potential duplication of beneficial ownership work in Ecuador.
Mongolia 
 
Company ownership is a matter of public interest for Mongolia’s mining sector, which  
plays an important role in the country’s economy. In 2022, Mongolia brought into effect a  
new law requiring companies to disclose their beneficial ownership information, including all 
beneficial owners of legal entities. At the same time, Mongolia committed to joining Opening 
Extractives and, at the programme’s launch, Mongolian government ministries, civil society  
and company representatives discussed the contribution that the programme could make. 
Opening Extractives is now supporting the Ministry of Justice to draft extractive transparency 
legislation to strengthen the legal framework for beneficial ownership disclosure in Mongolia. 
This bill is currently being discussed in Parliament. In collaboration with the Mongolia Data  
Club and Open Society Forum, Opening Extractives also contributed to capacity-building for 
journalists, data scientists and economists through workshops on beneficial ownership 
transparency, including for state-owned enterprises (SOEs). These workshops catalysed  
national public debate by aiding the publication of investigative articles on beneficial  
ownership challenges in the country.  
Mongolia’s EITI secretariat is also developing an online beneficial ownership disclosure system 
as a separate module to its existing online reporting platform. This new module will have data 
collection, processing and publishing features for a comprehensive and open data standard 
of beneficial ownership information. Expected to be functioning in early 2024, the new system 
will work in integration with various databases, such as the company registry at the General 
Registration Authority, the e-Tax system, the mining license and cadastre system and the  
income and asset declarations of PEPs at the Anti-Corruption Agency. Opening Extractives  
will provide technical assistance on designing this new system, coordinate with stakeholders  
and support in rolling out the system to designated users.

Liberia 
The Liberia Business Registry committed to building an open, public, online beneficial 
ownership register with data published in line with BODS in its 2020-2022 OGP National 
Action Plan. It was also one of the first two countries to commit to Opening Extractives, 
thereby affirming its commitment to ending anonymous company ownership. 
Opening Extractives first supported Liberia’s efforts in establishing a regulatory and legislative 
framework for beneficial ownership disclosures in early 2022. The programme researched  
and published a scoping assessment on the current state of play and identified practical,  
actionable recommendations for advancing beneficial ownership implementation. 
Since the publication of the scoping assessment, Liberia has made rapid progress to develop 
regulations and technology with the aim of streamlining collection and publication of data. 
A three-day multilateral peer exchange event for Anglophone African countries in July 2022 saw the 
team build technical capacity for staff at government agencies. There is ongoing dialogue with the 
National Steering Committee on beneficial ownership transparency, and workshops have been held 
for local stakeholders and civil society to share their experience of what beneficial ownership can 
enable. Through radio talk shows and town hall meetings, Opening Extractives is raising awareness 
of the importance of beneficial ownership transparency among the public. After publication  
of its new “Beneficial Ownership Regulations for Domestic Entities”, Liberia’s first central, public 
beneficial ownership register was launched in September 2023 by the Liberia Business Registry.

Zambia  
Since Zambia joined Opening Extractives in 2021, the programme has collaborated and  
offered technical assistance to the Patents and Companies Registration Agency (PACRA) 
through submissions on legislative amendments and capacity-building for technical staff.  
Over the past year, Opening Extractives has provided support on company disclosures, 
specifically through supporting the Ministry of Mines and Minerals Development, to  
strengthen beneficial ownership transparency mechanisms and build the capacity of the 
technical staff of PACRA in ten provinces. As a result of the support received, PACRA is 
in the process of undertaking legislative reforms through the corporate registry, including 
amendments to the Companies Act to strengthen provisions related to beneficial ownership  
for enhanced implementation and compliance. 
PACRA is also taking into consideration submissions by Opening Extractives that highlight  
gaps in the existing law. The programme held a high-level event for the Summit of Democracy 
with the Center for International Private Enterprise (CIPE), where panellists highlighted the need 
for clarity on how beneficial ownership data is used and shared. Opening Extractives and CIPE 
organised meetings to engage with the private sector to understand views held by the business 
community, company regulators and civil society who work to advance the implementation  
of beneficial ownership data collection in Zambia.
In late 2022, Opening Extractives contributed to national thought leadership  
on beneficial ownership disclosures in Zambia by engaging with a local law firm to identify  
gaps, inconsistencies and recommendations in line with the Open Ownership Principles, the 
Financial Action Task Force Recommendations 24 and 25 and the EITI Requirement 2.5 on 
beneficial ownership transparency. The report was well received by stakeholders in Zambia,  
as it provided a comprehensive review and analysis of beneficial ownership transparency laws, 
regulations and policies and offered practical recommendations on how the legislative,  
regulatory and policy framework could be improved. 
Opening Extractives also supported a CSO, the Centre for Trade Policy and Development,  
to conduct a project analysing the beneficial owners of the 24 biggest mining companies  
in the northern part of the country. The result of this collaboration was the establishment  
of a platform, built on existing beneficial ownership data from PACRA. In addition to enabling  
the public to understand who owns and benefits from companies, the platform can help  
improve due diligence processes and contribute towards domestic resource mobilisation  
by providing information on companies’ tax and revenues payments. 
“It would be useful to reframe how and why beneficial ownership 
transparency matters. Away from a narrative that focuses 
primarily on anti-money laundering benefits, and toward a 
narrative that highlights the many benefits which businesses 
can derive to de-risk their operations by accessing verified and 
accurate beneficial ownership information.”

Nigeria 
 
The most substantial work undertaken by Opening Extractives in Nigeria has been technical 
assistance, with a focus on the establishment of laws, systems and software for its beneficial 
ownership register. Opening Extractives has also trained civil society members and journalists 
in how to maximise the use of beneficial ownership data, as well as supporting a case study 
demonstrating the significance of beneficial ownership data in SOEs. 
Nigeria became the first African nation to release data to BODS format, and it was the first to 
launch a public extractive industry beneficial ownership register in 2019. In 2021, the Corporate 
Affairs Commission released a prototype of its economy-wide beneficial ownership disclosure 
system as an interim measure for accessing beneficial ownership information while it worked on a 
stand-alone public register. The register was launched in May 2023. It collects beneficial ownership 
data in line with BODS, providing high-quality structured data for government and non-government 
data users. Opening Extractives led a series of capacity-building training workshops, demonstrating 
how to analyse and use beneficial ownership data to improve natural resource advocacy and 
governance in Nigeria.
In Nigeria, Opening Extractives also supported Policy Alert, a local CSO, to develop a case study on 
beneficial ownership data use focusing on the trading of oil. The case study demonstrated that 
while there has been disclosure of beneficial ownership data by companies involved in oil trading 
in Nigeria, there are still considerable gaps that can give rise to risks of corruption and conflicts 
of interest. Policy Alert will continue to work with Opening Extractives, showing the importance of 
beneficial ownership data and supporting initiatives that improve natural resource governance in 
Nigeria aimed at combating illicit financial flows in the extractives industry.
Opening Extractives has further facilitated the collaboration between the Directorio Legislativo, a 
Latin American CSO, and Nigerian CSOs BudgIT and Follow Taxes, to develop an online platform. 
Using various sources of data to connect beneficial ownership data and data on PEPs, the Join 
the Dots (JtD) platform shows potential risks in the contract allocations of mining, oil and gas 
companies and reveals beneficial owners who are PEPs or relatives of PEPs. The JtD platform, 
initially piloted in Colombia in partnership with the EITI, was officially launched in Nigeria in 
September 2023. It is anticipated that civil society and journalists, among other stakeholders,  
will use it for investigative work in the extractive sector.
Philippines
Opening Extractives continues to provide technical guidance and support to the Philippines’ 
beneficial ownership registry for the extractive sector. The programme is helping to strengthen 
data disclosures through guidance on revisions of beneficial ownership data collection 
templates to ensure comprehensiveness and more usable data for analysis. It continues to 
help strengthen the legal framework for beneficial ownership transparency by providing advice 
on the drafting of beneficial ownership provisions under Executive Order No. 147, which aims 
to strengthen data collection procedures and enable the systematic disclosure of beneficial 
ownership data. 
Engagement at a policy level continues, building on presentations and workshops previously 
delivered to the Senate, policy makers and local reformers. Opening Extractives contributed to 
strengthening the government’s commitment to beneficial ownership transparency by providing 
inputs to the Department of Finance’s beneficial ownership commitments under the 2023 OGP 
National Action Plan. As an offshoot of the initial engagement with the legislative actors in the 
country, a webinar on beneficial ownership was co-facilitated by the Senate Economic Planning 
Office, the Department of Finance and Philippines EITI. These engagement efforts have led to 
increased awareness, and they have provided capacity-building to policy actors in the Senate of the 
Philippines and House of Representatives in developing options for legislative and policy reforms. 
Opening Extractives will continue to support awareness among legislators and law enforcement 
agencies on how beneficial ownership data can be used to complement the government’s broader 
reforms. It will engage further with legislative staff and parliamentarians to discuss opportunities 
to institutionalise beneficial ownership reforms in the country and address legal barriers to 
disclosure. Alongside this work at a policy level, Opening Extractives has organised technical 
training to civil society on how to source, interrogate and use beneficial ownership data.

Online workshop peer exchanges
Three online workshops with over 150 registered participants, held in late 2023,  
were an opportunity to demonstrate data analysis tools and show their use in 
creating and using structured beneficial ownership data. The workshop series was  
a way to engage with data users; show the importance of high-quality, well-structured 
data; and provide training on BODS, visualising data and analysing public registers. 
Noting the lack of data available in some contexts and the lack of disclosures in 
others, participants expressed that connecting international sources and tools for 
visualisations is extremely important for investigations. Peer exchange workshops 
play a crucial role in fostering collaboration, sharing knowledge and promoting growth 
among stakeholders in the programme. The second phase of the programme will 
focus on inter-agency collaborations in Ecuador and Francophone and Anglophone 
countries as part of its capacity-building efforts on beneficial ownership transparency.

Peer exchange
The EITI’s 2023 Global Conference was held in June 2023 in Dakar, Senegal.  
At the conference, Senegal chaired a peer exchange session for other countries 
in Francophone Africa, sharing its experience with beneficial ownership 
implementation and exchanging best practices with other governments. 
Opening Extractives has created platforms for government agencies to share 
emerging best practices from their beneficial ownership journeys, and it 
convenes regional peer exchange programmes for implementing countries. 
The Francophone Africa peer exchange focused on progress achieved in 
building strong legal frameworks across the region and shared successful 
examples of setting up beneficial ownership platforms and registers. It also 
highlighted that there is more work to be done to ensure the data is verified, 
collected in open format and used by a wider array of stakeholders. This session 
included representatives from all 15 countries implementing the EITI Standard 
in Francophone Africa, before broadening to a global perspective in the second 
half of the peer exchange.
Panel sessions 
There was strong interest in sessions at the 2023 EITI Global Conference 
which focused on beneficial ownership transparency. In addition to parallel 
sessions on data-driven approaches to corruption and on beneficial ownership 
transparency, a side event was hosted by Transparency International 
Australia. Discussions focused on why beneficial ownership is essential 
for maintaining business integrity across the transition minerals value 
chain as well as for ensuring a sustainable and just energy transition. 
Innovation talks  
Countries taking part in Opening Extractives shared groundbreaking and 
creative ways in which stakeholders have implemented beneficial ownership

Open Ownership Principles
2023 EITI Standard
Beneficial ownership reporting requirements have evolved significantly over the 
past decade. In 2013, the EITI Standard recommended that implementing countries 
establish public company registers, and in 2016, it introduced a landmark requirement 
for countries to disclose the beneficial owners of extractive companies from January 
2020 onwards. In June 2023, these provisions were further strengthened:
Lowering reporting thresholds
EITI countries are encouraged to adopt a threshold of 10% or lower for beneficial 
ownership disclosures to ensure that most or all ownership interests are disclosed and 
leave no room for hidden interests.
Exposing political connections
EITI countries are required to request full disclosure of PEPs who directly or indirectly 
hold an interest in a company, regardless of their level of ownership, which can help to 
identify conflicts of interest. 
Linking to the licensing process
EITI countries are encouraged to link publicly available license registers to government 
platforms containing information about legal and beneficial owners of oil, gas and 
mining companies. This can help regulators analyse who is behind the legal entities 
applying for, holding or transferring licenses.
Capturing control and ownership of SOEs, their 
suppliers and publicly listed companies
SOEs are required to disclose the state(s) owning or controlling the SOE; the level of 
ownership; and how ownership or control is exerted. They are also encouraged to 
disclose the identities and beneficial owners of their agents, intermediaries, suppliers or 
contractors for material transactions, which can help close avenues for corruption.
Disclosing legal ownership and ownership chains
EITI countries are required to disclose legal owners, while companies are encouraged to 
disclose their ownership structure, including the full chain of legal entities leading to the 
beneficial owner. This can help to map relationships and expose conflicts of interest.
Strengthening company due diligence
Both EITI supporting and reporting companies are expected to publish an anti-
corruption policy setting out how they manage corruption risk, including their use of 
beneficial ownership data, which can help tackle risks by identifying with whom they  
are doing business.

Objectives for phase two
As Opening Extractives plans the next phase of delivery – from January 2024 to December 2025 –  
it is adapting its focus and delivery approaches to take on board the independent evaluation findings 
and ensuring the programme continues to maximise impact. As a result of learnings from the midterm 
evaluation and the programme’s regular reviews, phase two focuses on four overarching goals:
Linking to the 
energy transition
Shaping and informing national and 
international debate on governance 
risks in the energy transition
In view of the growing importance of 
governance to the energy transition agenda, 
Opening Extractives is well placed to drive 
thought leadership and ensure beneficial 
ownership transparency is prominently 
placed within this agenda. This goal will 
be achieved in part through a sustained 
focus on documenting and communicating 
clear use cases and examples of beneficial 
ownership data use to mitigate governance 
risks in the energy transition and bring 
the programme’s in-country expertise to 
a global audience. The programme will 
continue to drive global discussions on how 
to navigate issues on beneficial ownership, 
including the need for public disclosure 
and addressing data privacy concerns.
Data quality  
and availability
Improving the availability and quality of beneficial 
ownership data for the extractive sector to 
meet Requirement 2.5 of the EITI Standard 
Leveraging its core strengths, Opening  
Extractives will double down on efforts to 
increase availability of beneficial ownership  
data where data is currently unavailable but 
there is a realistic possibility to make it available 
by 2025. Where information is already available 
but poor data quality or lack of public access 
are limiting use, the programme will focus on 
improving these factors. Lessons learned and 
tools used in supporting Opening Extractives 
countries to adhere to Requirement 2.5 will 
be cascaded to non-Opening Extractives 
countries to ensure the benefits of the 
programme are shared more widely.
Integration of 
beneficial ownership 
data use in licensing 
processes
Integrating the use of beneficial ownership data in 
processes for the application, award and holding 
of extractive licenses and contracts to mitigate 
governance risks through the energy transition 
Licensing has been highlighted as a key area for 
heightened governance risks associated with the energy 
transition. Embedding the use of beneficial ownership 
information into licensing processes is a priority.
Transfer of knowledge 
to local stakeholders
Building knowledge and transferring capacity to national, 
regional and local partners, institutions and champions
This goal responds to concrete needs from programme 
stakeholders as well as the need to ensure sustainability 
of the gains made under the programme once activities 
wind down. The focus on knowledge transfer responds 
to the needs of the EITI’s broader stakeholder base 
to learn from the Opening Extractives programme to 
improve global compliance with EITI Requirement 2.5, 
and it is essential for its sustainability and impact beyond 
the successful completion of each country’s activities. 
Through engagement with a wide range of stakeholders, 
Opening Extractives is in a strong position to keep 
delivering impactful work to help progress beneficial 
ownership transparency in a way that is adapted to 
the many contexts in which it operates. Leveraging 
Open Ownership’s technical expertise on beneficial 
ownership transparency with the EITI’s political reach, 
in-country networks and extractive sector expertise, and 
the leverage created through commitments to meet 
the EITI Standard, the programme will continue to drive 
and support concrete progress nationally. This will be 
done through facilitating the participation of national 
reformers in international debate and generating new 
evidence on frontier issues, such as the importance 
of beneficial ownership transparency in the transition 
minerals sector as the energy transition advances.
Lessons from phase one
Opening Extractives has pushed beneficial ownership up 
national agendas in countries implementing the programme, 
as well as bringing together stakeholders from within and 
beyond the extractive sector that would not usually engage 
with each other. The combination of high-level political 
engagement, plus technical capacity-building, awareness raising 
and peer exchange to increase stakeholders’ understanding 
of the key issues, are cited as important successes of the 
programme’s work so far. Stakeholders specifically noted 
the impact of bilateral and in-person peer exchanges.
Technical assistance is supporting countries to 
implement reforms and increase progress in meeting the 
EITI’s requirements on beneficial ownership. Technical 
assistance was highly valued by interviewees across 
the board. It proved particularly successful in countries 
with specific goals to meet EITI Requirement 2.5 on 
beneficial ownership, which could complement national 
reforms on anti-corruption through robust data collection 
and verification mechanisms, as well as data use.
Adaptation to context and change is essential to deliver  
impact that enables the programme to address challenges  
with ensuring continued impact at a national level 
when governments change or key officials leave 
their jobs. The programme’s adaptive approach was 
recognised as key to ensuring resilience of previous 
efforts and supporting sustainable reforms.
Using beneficial ownership data is a significant challenge,  
as the quality of data is often low. The evaluation 
suggests that a potential strategy is to focus on 
improving data quality and building the capacity of 
key agencies to use beneficial ownership data in 
countries where this issue is slowing progress.
